About me


My name is Eman Alajrami, a distinguished academic and researcher with a PhD in Computer Science, specialising in deep active learning for biomedical imaging in echocardiography. My PhD research was conducted in collaboration with the National Heart and Lung Institute, Imperial College London. With over a decade of academic experience and research in Palestine and the UK, I have been a part of renowned research projects, authored 15+ publications in AI and Computer Vision, and taught a range of courses in computer science and software engineering, supervised 40+ undergraduate students, and mentored students in competitions and startups. I am a member of ACM-W (Woman in ACM), BCS (The Chartered Institute for IT), WinSTIM and IntSaV research lab at the University of West London (UWL).
I am currently an HPL lecturer at UWL. Before joining UWL, I was the head of the Multimedia Department and a lecturer at the University of Palestine for more than 10 years.
In my free time, I enjoy cooking and spending more time with my family
